| Details   The Ogopogo is a lake monster said to inhabit Okanagan Lake in British Columbia, Canada. It is described as a serpentine creature with smooth dark skin, a large body thicker than a telephone pole, and a length of up to 50 ft. It is known to move at incredible speeds, coiling its body in vertical undulations and propelling itself with a powerful tail. The creature has its origins in First Nations folklore and is a prominent figure in the commercial symbolism and media representation of the region.
